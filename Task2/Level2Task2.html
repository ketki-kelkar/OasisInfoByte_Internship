Biography

Stephen Hawking, a renowned English theoretical physicist, left an indelible mark on the world of science with his groundbreaking contributions. Born on January 8, 1942, in Oxford, England, he pursued his studies at the University of Oxford and Trinity Hall, Cambridge, where he earned his Ph.D. in 1966. Despite being diagnosed with amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS) in the early 1960s, a condition that gradually immobilized him, Hawking displayed extraordinary determination, continuing his work in the realm of theoretical physics.


Hawking's pioneering work primarily centered on the profound complexities of general relativity and black holes. In 1971, he proposed the existence of mini black holes, compact yet massively dense objects governed by both relativity and quantum mechanics. One of his most notable contributions came in 1974, when he theorized that black holes emit subatomic particles, eventually leading to their evaporation. This groundbreaking insight bridged classical thermodynamics and quantum mechanics, revolutionizing our understanding of the universe's fundamental principles. His remarkable achievements earned him prestigious accolades, including fellowships from the Royal Society, the Lucasian professorship of mathematics at Cambridge once held by Isaac Newton, and notable honors like the Copley Medal and the U.S. Presidential Medal of Freedom. Through his seminal works, such as "A Brief History of Time" and "The Grand Design," coauthored with Leonard Mlodinow, Stephen Hawking's legacy continues to inspire generations and expand the horizons of human knowledge.

    Brief timeline of key events in the life of Stephen Hawking


  • January 8, 1942: Stephen Hawking is born in Oxford, England.

  • 1962: Hawking earns his Bachelor's degree in physics from University College, Oxford.

  • 1963: He begins his graduate studies at Trinity Hall, Cambridge.

  • 1965: Hawking marries Jane Wilde.

  • 1966: Completes his Ph.D. in theoretical physics from Cambridge.

  • 1967: Hawking's first child, Robert, is born.

  • 1960s: Diagnosed with amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS), a degenerative neuromuscular disease.

  • 1970s: Hawking works on his groundbreaking research in black holes and the nature of the universe.

  • 1974: Proposes the theory of black hole radiation, now known as Hawking radiation.

  • 1979: Becomes the Lucasian Professor of Mathematics at Cambridge, a position once held by Isaac Newton.

  • 1981: Publishes his popular science book, "The Universe in a Nutshell."

  • 1985: Loses his ability to speak due to complications from ALS.

  • 1988: Publishes "A Brief History of Time," a bestseller, making complex physics concepts accessible to the general public.

  • 1995: Divorces his first wife Jane Wilde.

  • 1997: Marries Elaine Mason, a former nurse.

  • 2007: Hawking divorces Elaine Mason.

  • 2009: Awarded the U.S. Presidential Medal of Freedom.

  • 2010: Coauthors "The Grand Design" with physicist Leonard Mlodinow.

  • 2018: Stephen Hawking passes away on March 14, at the age of 76, in Cambridge, England.
